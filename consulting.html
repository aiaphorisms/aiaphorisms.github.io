Consulting

I work with leadership teams on high-stakes technical problems in applied AI, evaluation, and large-scale systems—typically when delivery quality, cost, or execution risk matters. I operate at the Principal/Director level, bridging research, production reliability, and measurable business outcomes.

I take on a small number of consulting engagements each year, focused on delivering concrete improvements in applied AI systems. My work is hands-on and execution-heavy, aimed at teams that need deep technical help, fast iteration, and clear outcomes.

Engagements work best when the problem is concrete, the scope is bounded, and success can be defined upfront.

Examples of well-scoped engagements

  • End-to-end evaluation harness for AI products: designing and implementing a complete evaluation framework to measure quality, regressions, failure modes, and business impact for production AI systems, including LLM-based products.
  • Inference optimization and cost–quality tradeoffs: improving the cost-benefit profile of deployed models through inference-time optimization, architecture changes, or hybrid systems—without sacrificing product quality.
  • Domain-specific foundation or reasoning models: building or adapting smaller (<20B parameter) models—LLMs, LRMs, image generation, multimodal, or object-detection models—for a clearly defined domain or task, with a focus on deployability and ownership.
  • Classical ML where it still wins: designing and deploying traditional models (random forests, gradient-boosted trees, specialized clustering) where they provide superior reliability, interpretability, or cost-performance compared to deep learning alternatives.
  • Agentic AI systems with grounding: building full agent-based AI products that combine retrieval (RAG), multiple models, and tools—emphasizing observability, failure handling, and long-term maintainability.
  • Technical due diligence for acquisitions or strategic investments: deep evaluation of AI systems, data pipelines, model maturity, infra cost structure, and engineering risks to assess technical soundness, scalability, and integration feasibility beyond slideware and demos.

Engagement model and boundaries

To maximize delivery speed and reduce risk, my default approach is to design and implement standalone, composable components that integrate cleanly with your existing systems, rather than making invasive changes to production codebases.

  • Minimizes disruption to existing systems and teams
  • Reduces legal and IP entanglement around production code
  • Produces components that are easier to test, reason about, and maintain

When helpful, I will review your current architecture and codebase to assess fit and integration paths. If I believe an engagement is unlikely to deliver meaningful outcomes, I will say so early.

Collaboration

Effective work requires close collaboration. I typically ask for regular access to one senior engineer or architect who understands the system well and can answer detailed technical questions. Clear context and fast feedback are essential.

I prefer working with small, high-caliber teams. If skill gaps or resourcing issues materially block delivery, I will raise them early so they can be addressed.

Engagement structure

Most engagements begin with a short evaluation phase. This is a focused assessment of the problem space, constraints, existing systems, and delivery options.

  • Evaluation phase: one week (5 days), fixed engagement
  • Typical cost: $25,000

The evaluation phase ensures dedicated time, focus, and continuity. Follow-on work is scoped and priced after this phase, once delivery details and technical complexity are clear.

Follow-on engagements are typically 10 weeks or longer, depending on scope and technical difficulty.

Travel and compute costs, when applicable, are billed separately.

I do not take on projects unless I believe I can deliver meaningful results.

International and longer-term engagements

I am open to international engagements and, in some cases, longer-term work (e.g., six to nine months). These engagements work well when the scope, milestones, and success criteria are discussed and agreed upon early.

For longer engagements, I prioritize clear phase boundaries, periodic checkpoints, and explicit ownership transitions to ensure sustained progress and avoid open-ended commitments.

Engagement expectations

I work most effectively when discussions happen close to decision-making. As a result, I typically engage directly with executives or senior technical leaders who own scope, priorities, and outcomes.

To keep conversations substantive, I prefer that prospective collaborators review my technical writing or prior work before reaching out.

I do not participate in technical interviews, coding screens, or exploratory interview-style sessions. If we speak, the conversation is focused on a concrete problem and whether an engagement makes sense.
