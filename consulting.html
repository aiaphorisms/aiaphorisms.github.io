AI Product Architecture & Implementation Critical Review

I support executive teams with critical reviews of AI product architecture and implementation, focusing on whether systems are structurally sound, scalable, and controllable in production.

This work is designed for organizations where AI products are already live or nearing deployment, and where near-term decisions have long-term technical, financial, or organizational consequences.

Situations where this review is relevant

Engagements typically arise when:

  • AI systems perform well in demonstrations but show instability, drift, or degradation in production.
  • Model strategy (proprietary, third-party, or open-source) lacks clarity on tradeoffs, defensibility, or long-term control.
  • AI-related costs are increasing without proportional business impact.
  • Accountability for quality, regressions, or failures is unclear.
  • Leadership perceives elevated risk but lacks a fact-based view of where it originates.
  • Architectural choices have become implicit defaults rather than explicit decisions.
  • Existing metrics and dashboards do not support confident go / pause / rollback decisions.

What the review produces

A concise written executive document (memo or white paper) that addresses:

  • whether the current AI product architecture is fit for purpose,
  • where implementation risk exists, across technology and organization,
  • which decisions require immediate attention and which do not,
  • feasible options, tradeoffs, and exit paths,
  • quality, cost, and rollback criteria required for effective governance,
  • organizational structure and talent constraints affecting execution.

The objective is to enable clear, defensible decisions.

How the work is conducted

  1. Assessment
    Structured discussions with architects, engineering leadership, and product owners, combined with review of architecture, evaluation practices, cost structure, and ownership.
  2. Critical review
    Synthesis of findings into a written deliverable intended for C-level decision-making.
  3. Execution stewardship (optional)
    Continued involvement, if requested, to review designs, test assumptions, and ensure implementation remains aligned with original decisions. Delivery ownership remains with internal teams.

Where necessary, targeted diagnostics, experiments, or limited proofs-of-concept are conducted to validate assumptions and reduce uncertainty. These activities are used to inform decisions, not to replace delivery teams.

Scope boundaries

This work is appropriate when
  • there is a clear executive sponsor,
  • decision authority is defined,
  • leadership is prepared to adjust direction based on findings.
This work is not intended for
  • staff augmentation or delivery outsourcing,
  • open-ended exploratory research,
  • vendor-led tool selection,
  • RFP-driven evaluations.
