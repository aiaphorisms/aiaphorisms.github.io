About Me

I am an AI and engineering leader with over two decades of experience building and shipping production-grade systems across Amazon, eBay, Walmart, Adobe, Salesforce, and Microsoft — across search, recommendations, document AI, marketing measurement, and generative AI. I have shipped systems that moved revenue by nine figures and watched enterprise AI fail in ways that had nothing to do with the technology.

My work is grounded in first-principles thinking. I care about reliability, evaluation, and measurable outcomes — not process, presentation, or the performance of expertise.

Outside of work, I train in martial arts and write — poetry, essays, and creative pieces. I am drawn to how humans think and interact, how cultures shape each other, and how geopolitics quietly determines the texture of ordinary life. I believe advanced AI will eventually help human civilization transcend its own limitations — not by replacing human consciousness but by reflecting it clearly enough to accelerate its evolution toward what Sri Aurobindo called integral self-transcendence: knowledge, power, harmony and skill unified in service rather than domination.

That gap is what I write about on Inference & Power — AI strategy, organizational incentives, and the distance between leadership as taught and leadership as practiced. I also write on Forgotten Bose — Bengal politics, India AI policy, and literary essays.
