About Me

I am an AI and engineering leader with over two decades of experience building and shipping production-grade systems across Amazon, eBay, Walmart, Adobe, Salesforce, and Microsoft — across search, recommendations, document AI, marketing measurement, and generative AI. I have led organizations of eighty people, shipped systems that moved revenue by nine figures, and watched enterprise AI fail in ways that had nothing to do with the technology.

My work is grounded in first-principles thinking. I care about reliability, evaluation, and measurable outcomes — not process, presentation, or the performance of expertise.

I train in Muay Thai and boxing, study BJJ and Judo, and have practiced martial arts long enough to know that discipline is not about intensity — it is about structure that compounds over time. I sketch to think visually and write to think structurally. I read Russian literature, Sanskrit philosophy, and Bengali cultural writing. These are not hobbies separate from the work. They are how I reason — about systems, about organizations, about the gap between how things are described and how they actually function.

That gap is what I write about on Substack — AI strategy, organizational incentives, and the distance between leadership as taught and leadership as practiced.
